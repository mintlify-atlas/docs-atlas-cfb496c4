RSS Guard organizes your reading experience across three panels: a feed list on the left, an article list in the center, and an article preview pane on the right. Together they provide a focused, distraction-free environment for consuming news and podcast content.

## Interface overview

The main window is divided into three distinct panels that work together:

- **Feed list** — displays all your accounts, feeds, labels, queries, and special items such as the recycle bin.
- **Article list** — shows articles for whichever item is currently selected in the feed list.
- **Article preview** — renders the full content of the selected article, or summary information about the selected feed list item when no article is active.

The titlebar shows the total number of unread articles in square brackets so you always know your reading backlog at a glance. Two independent toolbars are available — one for the feed list and one for the article list — each configurable with the actions you use most.

## Adding your first account

When you launch RSS Guard for the very first time, the **Add account** dialog opens automatically. Here you choose the type of service you want to connect to.

For a standard feed reader that consumes RSS, RDF, Atom, or JSON feeds, select the **RSS/RDF/ATOM/JSON** option. Other entries in the list correspond to hosted services such as Nextcloud News, Inoreader, and Feedly.

<Note>
Each account type exposes its own set of account-specific actions. These are accessible through the account's submenu in the feed list, so the options you see will differ between a local RSS account and a cloud-synced service.
</Note>

## Adding feeds

Once an account exists you can add any number of feeds to it. RSS Guard supports automatic feed discovery, so you rarely need to hunt down a raw feed URL.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the add feed dialog">
    In the menu bar, select **Feeds → Add item → Add a new feed**. The feed discovery dialog will open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the website URL">
    Type the URL of any website you want to follow — for example `https://www.hltv.org`. RSS Guard will contact the site and automatically discover all available feed sources.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a discovered feed">
    Choose from the list of feeds that RSS Guard found on the page and confirm your selection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="(Optional) Use the advanced dialog">
    If you already know the direct feed URL, switch to the advanced dialog. Enter the URL into the **Source** field and click **Fetch it now**. RSS Guard will download the feed metadata and populate all remaining fields automatically. In the vast majority of cases no further configuration is needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
In 99% of cases you only need to paste the feed URL into the **Source** field and click **Fetch it now** — RSS Guard fills in everything else.
</Tip>
