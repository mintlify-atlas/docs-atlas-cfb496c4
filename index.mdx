RSS Guard is an open-source, cross-platform desktop feed reader built on top of the Qt framework. It supports RSS, ATOM, RDF, JSON, iCalendar, and Sitemap feed formats, integrates with popular online feed services, and includes a built-in podcast/media player — all in a single lightweight application.

## Get started quickly

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download RSS Guard">
    Head to the [Downloads](/downloads) page and grab the installer or portable package for your operating system.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your first account">
    When you launch RSS Guard for the first time, choose **RSS/RDF/ATOM/JSON** for local feeds or pick one of the supported online services.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add feeds">
    Use **Feeds → Add item → Add a new feed** and enter a website URL. RSS Guard will auto-discover available feeds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read and customize">
    Browse your articles, set up [labels](/features/labels), configure [notifications](/features/notifications), and optionally write [article filters](/features/filters) to automate your reading workflow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported platforms

RSS Guard runs on **Windows 7+**, **GNU/Linux**, **macOS 10.14+**, **BSD**, and **OS/2**. See the full [supported operating systems](/supported-os) page for details.
