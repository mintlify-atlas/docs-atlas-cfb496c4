RSS Guard is an open-source, cross-platform desktop feed reader and podcast player developed on top of the [Qt framework](http://qt-project.org). It is designed to be fast and lightweight while offering enough depth for power users. RSS Guard reads feeds in standard formats such as RSS, ATOM, RDF, and JSON, integrates with popular online feed services, and can play audio and video directly through its built-in media player — no external applications required.

## Key capabilities

  <Card title="Feed reading" icon="rss" href="/supported-readers">

  <Card title="XMPP & Gemini" icon="comments" href="/features/xmpp">

  </Card>

## Key highlights

RSS Guard supports the following feed formats out of the box:

- **RSS**, **ATOM**, **RDF**, **JSON** (JSONFeed)
- **iCalendar** — calendar event feeds
- **Sitemap** — including Sitemap index discovery and compressed sitemaps
- **Gemini** — feeds fetched securely over the Gemini protocol

It runs on all major desktop operating systems:

- **Windows** 7 and later
- **GNU/Linux** — mainstream distributions, PinePhone, and Inkbox e-readers
- **macOS** 10.14 and later
- **BSD** — FreeBSD, OpenBSD, NetBSD, and others
- **OS/2** — ArcaOS and eComStation

<Note>
  RSS Guard uses a plugin-based architecture. Each supported online service (Feedly, Tiny Tiny RSS, Nextcloud News, and so on) is implemented as a built-in plugin. The [plugin API](/contrib/papi) is also available to developers who want to add new service integrations.
</Note>
